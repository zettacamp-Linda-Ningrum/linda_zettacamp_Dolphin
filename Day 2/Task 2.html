♦ Invalidite

Author : Faradita
Publication Year : 2018
       One of the things Dewa rarely does besides smiling is attending class, but this morning was different because now he was at campus with a disheveled face carrying a backpack containing only a camera and cigarettes while climbing the stairs lazily. This is because it is not the custom of a god to get up early. Drowsiness that can not be prevented accompanies his steps. He yawned, scratched the back of his neck then his stomach hit something. More precisely, he bumped into someone accompanied by the sound of something falling on the floor following someone's scream. That someone is the Dusk Lamp. Dewa often feels annoyed by Pelita who always responds to his rude attitude with a smile. Especially when the girl is forced to arrange her life. Without realizing the consequences that might hurt himself, Dewa starts a game to conquer Pelita because the girl is a complete package for Dewa. He will use this girl to win a bet and hurt her step cousin.
  • Book's Review:
    1. "A simple story with a very extraordinary demeanor! Pelita exists as its name suggests to Dewa and readers. Absorbing the meaning of perfection in a deficiency. So recommended!" — Kokoh, an author.
    2. "Invalidite is the complete package! There are parts that make me fly, but there are also parts that make me fall, get sick and end up crying. Invalidite is a lesson that is a shame to miss. Again, Kak Fara has succeeded in playing with the hearts of readers here." — Anindya Frista, an author.
    3. "One of the best romance stories I've ever read. Beautiful, romantic, sweet, rich in taste, a beautiful story that will melt your heart." — Wiwi Suyanti, an author.

♦ Paper Towns

Author : John Green
Publication Year : 2015
       When Margo Roth Spiegelman takes Quentin Jacobsen out in the middle of the night, dressed as a ninja and with a long list of plans for revenge, the boys follow suit. Margo really likes to make complicated plans, and until now he has always acted alone. Meanwhile, Q, Q is happy to finally be close to the girl he can only love from afar. Until morning came and Margo disappeared again. The girl who has always been an enigma is now a mystery. However, there are some clues. Everything is for Q. And the boy realizes that the closer he gets to Margo, the less he knows this girl.
  • Book's Review:
    1. "I like everything about Paper Towns except for its premise and central romance. Yes, I know those sort of seem like dealbreakers, but the things I liked about Paper Towns marginally outweighed the things I didn't like. Marginally." — Max Weiss, Baltimore Magazine.
    2. "A bittersweet teen film that has much to offer even those of us who have, in theory, grown up." — Helen O'Hara, GQ Magazine (UK).
    3. "Paper Towns is particularly good at pinpointing that certain point in teenage male friendships where the guys are getting older, but they still sometimes resort to silly voices and goofy humor when the girls aren't around." — Richard Roeper, Chicago Sun-Times.

♦ The Fault in Our Stars

Author : John Green
Publication Year : 2014
       Even if a medical miracle shrinks the tumor and keeps Hazel alive for a few more years, Hazel Grace remains hopeless. Hazel felt there was no point in living in the world anymore. However, when fate brings her together with Augustus Waters at the Support Group for Children with Cancer, Hazel's life changes 180 degrees. Enlightening, bold and moving, The Fault in Our Stars brilliantly explores the humor, the suspense, and the tragedy of life and love.
  • Book's Review:
    1. "The Fault In Our Stars could indeed become this generation's Love Story, mildly implausible, but genuinely moving." — Jim Slotek, Toronto Sun.
    2. " It's a carpe diem love story that overcomes its own faults, and earns its tempered hopefulness, by maturely recognizing that love and pain are equal, and often intertwined, parts of life." — Nic Schager, SF Weekly.
    3. "I think you will taste the stars when you see The Fault in Our Stars. It is a love story for our time that encourages us to remember, Love is stronger than death." — Sr. Rose Pacatte, National Catholic Reporter.

♦ Dignitate

Author : Hana Margaretha
Publication Year : 2017
       Alfi, a handsome man with all the abilities he has, is a genius, a champion, respected by many people, and able to conquer many women's hearts. Unfortunately, he is notoriously fierce. He always hurts people with his words. sarcastic, spicy, full of criticism and shock. He is also known as a sadist, especially with people who disturb his peace. However, there is one drawback that Alfi has. He had never been in a relationship, had never tasted the bitter sweetness of falling in love with a woman. Only Alana Caroline, a tiny girl who is very sweet and cheerful, is able to destroy Alfi's defense not to give hearts to women. Unfortunately, Alfi is too proud to confess his feelings for Alana.
  • Book's Review:
    1. "I really like Alfi's character who is so adorable! Fierce but makes you miss him! Prestige too! His silent attention is what makes me smile to myself, I really like it!" — Vallerina Angel, a Goodreads reader.
    2. "In this novel I like the cover, the characters are also appropriate, the plot used is coherent even though at the beginning the story seems a bit long-winded but here it is covered by elements of story humor, and lastly I also like that the conflict in this novel is difficult to predict." — Katharina Permatasari, a Goodreads reader.
    3. "The writing is good, I can still finish it to the end. But for story plots like this, it's been too many times." — Eliesa, a Goodreads reader.

♦ The Star and I

Author : Ilana Tan
Publication Year : 2021
       "I see the world in your eyes and the future in your smile..." Since childhood, Olivia Mitchell wanted to know who her real parents were. So when he got the chance to work in New York, he jumped at it without hesitation. However, finding someone without a name in a city the size of New York was impossible. Now Olivia's work contract is about to end, and Olivia refuses to return to England before successfully tracking down her biological parents. That meant he had to find a new job quickly so he could stay in New York. As if all that wasn't confusing enough, Olivia is suddenly reunited with Rex Rankin, her childhood friend and failed first love, who appears to offer her help.
  • Book's Review:
    1. "The only thing I like about this novel is the subplot about Olivia's birth parents. The rest tend to be flat and still use the same formula. Honestly, haven't enjoyed much since In a Blue Moon, but wish things had changed here. Unfortunately, his hopes have not been achieved." — Laili Mutamimmah, an author.
    2. "After 5 years, it's finally time to enjoy Ilana Tan's newest novel again. And I immediately fell in love with her descriptive writing style which I think is indeed the strength of Ilana Tan's storytelling style, especially when she tells about Broadway and the ins and outs of NYC." — Meiliana Kan, a Goodreads reader.
    3. "The book is very well written and the details are good, it feels very real, the atmosphere that the author has built makes us enter into the story. Even though the language is exactly like a translated novel, we can still see the simplicity in the sentences like in local teenlit novels." — Yunita Taman, a Goodreads reader.
